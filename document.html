Documents required for purchasing property

Sale Agreement : Details the terms, property description, and agreed price between buyer and seller.


Local Authority Registration Certificate : Proof of the property’s record in municipal documents.


Property Tax Payment Evidence : Confirms all due taxes by the previous owner have been settled.


Loan Clearance certificate : Verifies no pending loans on the property, crucial for financing applications.


Habitation Certificate : Issued post-construction, certifying the building’s compliance with plans and readiness for occupancy.


Bank Loan Statement : Shows any outstanding loans, ensuring transparency in financial obligations.


No-Objection Certificate (NOC) : Required from various departments, confirming no legal impediments to construction.


Approved Construction Plan : Guarantees adherence to sanctioned construction plans by the developer.


Authorised Representative Document : Confirms the legal authority of any representative acting on the owner’s behalf.


Documents Required for Selling Property

Sale Deed and Title Deed : The primary document for transferring property ownership to the buyer.


Mutation Record : Important for updating government records with the new owner’s details.


Power of Attorney : Necessary for sellers not physically present, allowing representatives to act on their behalf.


Building Plan and Occupation Certificate : Important for updating government records with the new owner’s details.


Clearance Certificate : From the builder or society, depending on the property’s registration status.


Allotment Letter : Issued by the housing society or authority, confirming the property’s allocation to the seller.


Sale Agreement : Outlines the terms and conditions of the sale, forming the basis for the Sale Deed.


Possession Letter : Marks the transfer of property ownership from the seller to the buyer.


Up-to-date Tax Receipts : Evidence of settled property taxes, essential for legal and loan processes.


Encumbarance Certificate : Shows the property’s free title, indicating no outstanding legal issues or debts.


Completion Certificate : Verifies the property’s construction as per the sanctioned plan, necessary for legal occupancy.


Occupancy Certificate : Proof that the property is ready and legal for habitation.


Local Registration Certificate : Specific to certain regions, like Karnataka’s Khata certificate, confirming property tax details.


Documents required for Proving Ownership of Land

Land Sale Deed : Validates the seller’s right to transfer the property.


Government Revenue Records : Official records showing property details and ownership.


Land Entitlement Certificate : Official document proving ownership and the right to sell.


Lease Documentation : For government-leased properties, confirming the lease terms.


Utility Bill Receipts : Serve as additional proof of ownership and property responsibility.


Inheritance or Gift Documentation : Legal papers confirming property transfer through inheritance or as a gift.
